# Whipped Cream

## Ingredients
* Heavy cream or heavy whipping cream (cold, 1 cup yields about 2 cups whipped cream)
* Sugar: powdered/confectioners’ sugar is preferred for smoothness; granulated sugar or alternatives like maple syrup or honey can also be used (1–2 tablespoons per cup of cream)
* Vanilla extract (1 teaspoon, optional)
* Optional stabilizers: a pinch of salt, gelatin, or mascarpone for richer texture and longer-lasting peaks. 

## Equipment
* Electric mixer (handheld or stand) or whisk
* Large mixing bowl (glass or metal preferred)
* Chilled bowl and beaters improve whipping efficiency. 

Instructions
* Chill your tools and cream: Place the bowl and beaters in the freezer for 10–20 minutes; keep cream cold from the fridge. 
* Measure cream into chilled mixing bowl and beat with whisk on mediumm speed until it begins to thicken
* Add sugar and vanilla
* Continue whipping until the cream reaches the desired consistency.
* * Soft peaks: Cream clings but peaks curl over; ideal for folding into desserts or coffee.
* * Medium peaks: Peaks hold shape but slightly droop; perfect for topping pies or cakes.
* * Stiff peaks: Peaks stand straight; suitable for piping or layered desserts. 
* * Avoid over-whipping: Over-whipped cream separates into butter and buttermilk. If this happens, gently fold in a small amount of cold cream to restore smoothness.

# Tips and Variations
* Flavoring: Add cocoa, coffee, liqueurs, citrus zest, or spices for flavored whipped cream. 
* Stabilizing: Use powdered sugar (contains cornstarch), gelatin, or mascarpone to help whipped cream hold its shape longer. 
* Freezing: Pipe or spoon onto a parchment-lined tray, freeze, then store in an airtight container for up to 3 months; thaw in the fridge before use.

# Storage
Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for 1–3 days; gently re-whip if it loses volume. 
Freezer: Freeze in portions for later use; thaw and lightly re-whip to restore texture. 

## Notes
Homemade whipped cream is versatile and enhances desserts like pies, cakes, pancakes, hot chocolate, and fresh fruit. With just a few ingredients and proper technique, it’s richer, fresher, and more customizable than store-bought alternatives.
